Motherboard

The mobo is where various components are installed

CPU

The CPU, or Central Processing Unit, is where and how a computer executes and make calculations. A modern, high-range CPU consists of millions, in some cases, just over a billion transistors. A transistor is used in a digital circuit as a switch, turning things on and off i.e. turning pixels on and off when moving a mouse.

RAM

RAM is used to store information intended to be used in the near future and is used by running applications(i.e. browser, games, etc.)

Hard Drive

A Hard Disk Drive "writes" information on a spinning disk to store data and "reads" information on the disk to recall information. The magnetic disk records data through a magnetic-arm like material. The pattern that data is stored is similar to a recod, the data is stored in a circular path.

BUS

BUS is where components interact. There are internal and external buses. An internal bus connects all internal components, such as the CPU to motherboard where gold contacts, depending on the manufacturer, interacts with the bus on the motherboard.

Keyboard, Mouse, and Monitor

Keyboard

\

A keyboard sends a signal to the computer when each key is pressed, in a membrane keyboard, an electric circuit is completed when a key is pressed, the information is directed to the processor where it interacts with what you are typing.

Mouse

A mouse is the most popular pointing device for interacting with the computer. A mouse uses a light source(LED, laser, etc.) along with photobodies in order to detect movement. This information is forwarded to the CPU and interacts with the corresponding program.

Monitor

The monitor is where all graphics and video are displayed. The monitor is composed of different ratios of pixel i.e. 1920x1080.
